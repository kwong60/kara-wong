What is BDH?

The Brown Daily Herald (BDH) is the award-winning student newspaper of Brown University, established in 1866.

The Herald is a cross-platform publication with content available in print, online, and, most recently, through a mobile application with over 1000 downloads.

Pain Points

Digital Presence: With print quickly going out of date, the Herald aims to reach more of their audience by meeting them where they are — on their phones.

Breaking News: With the unpredictable pace of current events, delivering breaking news through traditional print is challenging. The Herald needs a channel to share instant, real-time updates with the student population.

Section Preferences

To cater to all audiences, I built a section preferences page to allow the user to prioritize the topics most relevant to their interests.

I used a draggable flatlist to maximize individualized control, where dragging any component would reorder the sections in the horizontal scroll menu and the newsfeed.

I also deleted a feature I had originally implemented where users could toggle any section's visibility.

The main concern was that those less technologically literate, like older users who want to see their children's or grandchildren's work, may accidentally eliminate a section and not know how to retrieve it.

Filters

After our official launch, we received user feedback that the search filters were difficult to find. I was assigned to implement this feature from end-to-end, brainstorming, designing, and coding a component that would enhance discoverability.

I ultimately separated the types of filters and displayed each using bottom drawers to simplify the multi-screen navigation.

Reflection

Flexibility: Readily letting go of initial ideas is pivotal to exploring potentially better and more accessible solutions.

Know Your Audience: Your user base will likely contain people who do not share your skillset or intuition. Designs must be considerate of all users.

Active Listening: User feedback is crucial to product improvement. It is not just about hearing users, but genuinely understanding their needs and frustrations — even when the feedback contradicts your assumptions.
