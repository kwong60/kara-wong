Introduction

Newton, MA — birthplace of the Fig Newton, location of the Boston Marathon's notorious Heartbreak Hill, and city that I proudly call home.

The existing government website does not do justice for Newton's unique character, so I set out to design a clean and informative interface to better reflect the city's vibrant culture and New England charm.

Pain Points

Organization: From its color contrast errors to its cluttered layout, Newton's original homepage is unintuitive and visually displeasing. With this website's express purpose to provide information, efficient navigation is crucial.

Responsiveness: On smaller screens, the homepage loses all unique qualities and even access to some resources. Residents in need of on-the-go assistance should have equal functionality and clarity.

Visual Style Guide

As Newton is known as "The Garden City," my visual style guide draws inspiration from natural and earthy tones with a specific emphasis on color accessibility.

Mock-Ups

Pulling from my Visual Style Guide, I designed mock-ups across varying screen sizes, tailoring specific features for optimal accessbility on laptops, tablets, and phones.

Responsive Design

Using HTML/CSS and JavaScipt, I translated this mock-up into a static front-end prototype. You can interact with elements by hovering over them.

With a cohesive color palette, intuitive navigation, and optimized layout for different screen sizes, this redesign ensures a more inclusive and user-friendly experience for all visitors.

Reflection

Power of Simplicity: By stripping away visual clutter and focusing on essential content, I was able to create a cleaner and more approachable interface that was easier to navigate.

Mobile-First Thinking: Rather than adapting a desktop design downward, it is important to keep mobile constraints in mind from the beginning. Designing responsively from the ground up allows for a more consistent user experience.
