What is Partiful?

Partiful is a free party planning platform that streamlines the process of creating, managing, and sharing events.

As both a web and mobile application, Partiful's main focus is user-friendliness and simplicity, implementing features to easily track RSVPs, poll guests, and send reminders.

Pain Points

Formality: The event creation process can be too superfluous for smaller parties. RSVPing to one host feels too official, and users often want to bypass features like choosing a theme or adding a cover photo.

Brainstorming: Users tend to make Partiful invites only after the details have been finalized. Partiful wants to capture users earlier in the planning phase.

Sketches

To brainstorm, we hand-sketched some elements we felt would best project a more casual atmosphere.

While familiarizing ourselves with Partiful, we found some existing features, like Party Genie and Google Calendar Sync, that could further contribute to the platform's ease of use. To enhance discoverability, we experimented with bringing these features to the forefront of the user flow.

Wireframes

From our sketches, we agreed on an intermediate screen to separate formal events from casual hangouts. To streamline the planning process, we also incorporated a calendar-adjacent interface so that guests could more easily compromise on a time.

We combined these features into a low-fidelity prototype on Balsamiq to send to our Partiful contact.

Hi-Fi Prototypes

From our client, we received feedback that the home page and multi-screen process may still be too crowded and overwhelming for a casual hangout.

For our high-fidelity prototype, we focused on simplifying the calendar functionality and resizing elements based on priority. We also developed two parallel user flows to better align with the differing expectations and habits of our target audience.

Reflection

Iterative Design: Working and reworking prototypes through multiple rounds of brainstorming and feedback maximizes both user and client satisfaction.

Fail Fast: Quickly identifying what does not work through early-stage testing allows us to pivot without investing too much time in the wrong direction.

Team Collaboration: Building off of each other's ideas, giving and receiving feedback openly, and leveraging each team member’s strengths allowed us to build a more thoughtful and user-centered solution.
